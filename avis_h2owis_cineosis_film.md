# AVIS → h₂ÓU̯IS
### The Fable Has No Original

**Seed:** Schleicher's fable, *Avis akvāsas ka*, 1868–2013 · **Mode:** CINEOSIS_FILM · **Duration:** 2:30 · **Render:** poetic-cybernetic cinema

---

## TL;DR LOGLINE

The oldest sentence composed in a language no one ever spoke is rewritten ten times across 150 years; inside the text, a shorn sheep watches a man wear its wool and drive its neighbors — and at the end of every version, flees into the same open field. The film cuts laterally across reconstructions instead of forward through story. Shearing and rewriting are one gesture. The "original" is the single frame that can never be shot.

## TEMPORAL ENGINE

**PAST — origin / wound.** The reconstructed pastoral: shorn sheep, three horses, heavy cart, a man in a wool mantle. It never occurred as a present. It is emitted backward by every revision. The wound: the sheep's covering is on the master's back.

**PRESENT — pressure.** The desk, 1868–2013. Ten reconstructions overwrite one sentence; diacritics gather on the words like burrs on fleece; every gain in precision pushes the origin further out of reach. One version (Kortlandt) even forks itself into before/after. Nib = shears.

**FUTURE — haunting.** The unpublished eleventh version, already implied. The one invariant across all ten texts is the final clause — the sheep flees into the field (*agrom* / *pleʕnom*: even the field gets renamed). The future is that blank field: the page not yet reconstructed.

**ENGINE LOGIC.** Reconstruction runs causality in reverse — the future revises the past. Crystal seed: the fable of the shorn animal travels on media descended from its skin (fleece → felt → sheepskin parchment → paper). The carrier is the wound.

## FORM

- Single channel, 2:1, 2 min 30 s, 12 beats.
- Palette: chalk white, graphite, ink black. One warmth only — oil-lamp amber, reserved for the desk layer.
- Three grain regimes: silver-gelatin (past), paper fiber and ink bleed (present), phosphor and scan-line (future).
- Language on screen is always physical matter: wet ink, strike-throughs, letterpress ghosts, punched dot-matrix. No floating type.

## ABC MODEL

**A — Actor / Affect.** The Sheep = the fable itself: a body whose covering (wool / letters) is repeatedly removed and refitted by its masters (the man / the philologists). Governing affect: *k̂ḗr moj aghnutór* — "the heart tightens in me" — the ache of watching your own substance worn by the one who drives you.

**B — Body / Behavior.** Fleece, iron hand-shears, sheepskin parchment, steel nib and gall ink, lead type, reel-to-reel tape, oscilloscope glass, dot-matrix head, rusted wire fence, chalk turf. Gestures: shearing, hauling, striking through, pinning, pronouncing-and-failing, fleeing.

**C — Continuity / Crystal.** Parallel-text time. Ten versions of one sentence held simultaneously; the edit moves sideways across versions while story time advances only inside the pastoral. Crystal face: actual = the shorn animal / virtual = its written reconstruction — each mirrors the other, and no side is designated source.

## MOTIF SYSTEM

1. **FLEECE → VELLUM.** Wool on the body → wool as garment → skin as page. Trajectory: full fleece falling (past) → scraped writing surface, worn paper (present) → one tuft snagged on wire (future).
2. **THE LARYNGEAL h₂.** The mark for a sound no living throat has produced. Renders as breath-fog, as a gap in playback, as a punched hole. Trajectory: absent (1868) → superscript intrusion (1939–1979) → glyph colonizing every word (1997–2013) → pure unvoiced breath (final frame).
3. **THE WAGON / THE APPARATUS.** *gʷr̥hₓúm wóĝhom*, the heavy load: scholarship as haulage; diacritics harness the words. Trajectory: oak cart on chalk → card drawer and tape reel on a library cart → rust.
4. **THE FIELD (agrom / pleʕnom).** The open ground every version flees into. Grass and set type converge until furrow and sentence cannot be told apart. The field is the unwritten page.

## SOUND MAP

- **Three beds.** Chalk wind (past) · lamp hiss and paper (present) · tape transport and phosphor whine (future). Beds crossfade at layer changes; full silence only at B9.
- **The choir.** Ten readers, one per reconstruction, entering in publication order 1868→2013; phonology hardens across the film — open unmarked vowels → laryngeal breath-hits → glottalic and pharyngeal stops. An eleventh synthesized voice owns the future layer and is cut off mid-clause at B12.
- **One percussion cell.** Shears → nib → dot-matrix head → hooves: the same rhythmic figure migrating across materials at ~92 bpm, a horse's walking gait. Every hard cut lands on it.
- **Rule of h₂.** Every laryngeal in the spoken text is replaced by an unvoiced breath-hit close to the microphone. By B12 the breath is the only voice left in the film.

## EDIT MAP

- **Lateral montage.** The default cut moves sideways across versions of the same instant, not forward in time. Only the pastoral layer is permitted narrative progression.
- **Percussive sync.** All hard cuts land on the migrating shears/nib/print-head/hoof cell.
- **Rationed dissolves.** Two only (B3 and B11), reserved for actual↔virtual exchange. Everything else cuts.
- **Palindrome hinge.** B7 (the wall of versions) is the axis: B12 answers B1, B10 answers B2 (flight undoes shearing), B11 answers B3 (aerial page answers desk page).
- **Text discipline.** Words appear only as photographed matter — wet ink, strike-through, carving, punch — never as overlay.

## FINAL IMAGE

The film ends inside its first frame, transformed. Same chalk hillside, same low light as B1 — but where the sheep's eye once held the three horses, the frame now holds a tuft of wool snagged on rusted wire and, carved shallow into the fence post, "h₂ówis" over an older word worn illegible. The eye that perceived has become the residue that is read. Opening image: a body seeing. Final image: a page remembering. Last sound: the laryngeal alone — one unvoiced breath — then black.
